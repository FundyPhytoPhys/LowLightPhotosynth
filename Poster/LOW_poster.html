Background

Light energy absorbed by the photosynthetic pigments of PSII (primarily chlorophyll-a) is distributed variably among three pathways1:
I. Photochemistry (photochemical quenching of ChlF)
II. Dissipation as heat (non-photochemical quenching of ChlF)
III. Re-emission at longer wavelengths as fluorescence (ChlF)

Occasionally, recombination may result in the loss of charge separation, desynchronizing the s-state cycle and dampening the periodicity.

Objective

Use a single-turnover variable chlorophyll fluorescence (ST-ChlF) approach to evaluate the loss of synchronization of the s-state cycle at different frequencies of photon delivery and temperatures.

Future Directions

The accepted lower limit of the photic zone is 1% of surface irradiance, equivalent to 1 photon reaching a PSII every ~0.03 seconds4. However, some arctic diatoms may achieve net C uptake when 1 photon only reaches a PSII every 0.6-60 seconds. Comparing the duration of s-state synchronicity of temperate and arctic diatoms may help explain how arctic diatoms exploit extremely low light.
